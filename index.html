EARLY KICK DETECTION SYSTEM

KRITI VYAS
PETROLEUM ENGINEER
OBJECTIVE
To detect early kick conditions by monitoring pressure changes in the wellbore and providing timely alerts.
PRINCIPLE
A kick occurs when formation pressure exceeds hydrostatic pressure, causing a sudden rise in wellbore pressure, which can be detected.
APPLICATION
This model is used to demonstrate early kick detection based on pressure variation in drilling operations. It helps in understanding well control concepts and visualizing how pressure changes during abnormal conditions. The system also serves as an educational and training tool, allowing users to observe real-time data and learn safety measures required to prevent blowouts.
HOW IT WORKS
  • Water chamber represents wellbore
  • Balloon acts as kick source
  • Air pumped → balloon expands
  • Expansion causes pressure change
  • Sensor measures pressure
  • Data shown as readings & graph
  • Sudden rise indicates kick
  • Sudden drop indicates loss
